About Diabetic Retinopathy

Diabetic retinopathy is a diabetes complication that affects the eyes. It's caused by damage to the blood vessels of the light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye (retina).

Severity Levels:

  • No DR: No signs of diabetic retinopathy
  • Mild: Small areas of balloon-like swelling
  • Moderate: More blood vessels are blocked
  • Severe: Many more blood vessels are blocked
  • Proliferative: Most severe stage with new blood vessels
